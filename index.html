Why Writing

The bottleneck is not reading the rule. It is writing the institutional story.

Regulatory writing is where rules, evidence, institutional reality, and review expectations collide. The rule may be stable. The institution is not.

Rules do not arrive as finished narrative. Teams still have to convert requirements into a coherent story.

Evidence lives across the institution. Policies, controls, minutes, runbooks, MIS, and prior drafts rarely line up cleanly on their own.

Gaps surface late. Unsupported claims and unresolved judgments often appear only after a large amount of manual writing effort.

Writing Process

One governed writing process, end to end.

  1. 01

    Read the rule and compile the requirement

    Turn governing sources into clear writing obligations, expected evidence, and destinations in the narrative.

  2. 02

    Understand the institution and evidence base

    Build a grounded picture of the institution from policies, controls, records, operating evidence, and documentary context.

  3. 03

    Benchmark against prior KPMG deliverables

    Bring forward strong prior work product, reusable narrative patterns, and learned review expectations as benchmark intelligence.

  4. 04

    Draft the narrative and show what is still missing

    Produce the first draft, carry forward explicit uncertainty, and surface the gaps and judgment calls that still need attention.

KPMG Advantage

KPMG precedent turns writing into a compounding capability.

What makes this more than a generic drafting platform is the ability to draw on prior KPMG deliverables as benchmark intelligence.

Prior deliverables become precedent. Existing narratives, working papers, and review structures become reusable patterns instead of archived artifacts.

Benchmarking becomes part of the writing process. Drafts can be shaped against what strong work has looked like before, not just against abstract rules.

Institutional memory compounds. Each cycle strengthens the firm’s ability to produce defensible writing faster and with clearer visibility into what still needs judgment.
