Why Teams Stall

The bottleneck is stitching rules, evidence, and institutional reality into a defensible story.

Regulatory writing still breaks down because the narrative has to be rebuilt by hand from scattered sources every time the institution changes.

Rules do not arrive in narrative form. They have to be compiled into writing obligations, evidence asks, and destinations in the document.

Evidence is institution-wide. Policies, controls, minutes, runbooks, MIS, and prior drafts rarely line up cleanly on their own.

Gaps appear too late. Unsupported claims and unresolved judgments often surface after too much drafting effort has already been spent.

How It Starts

The writing process begins with requirements, evidence, and prior work already in view.

Teams do not start from a blank page. They start from compiled requirements, mapped institutional evidence, prior KPMG precedent, and a clear view of what still needs support.

01

Requirements and evidence in one place

The draft begins with compiled obligations and mapped source material, so the writing starts from what the rule requires and what the institution can support.

02

Prior work carried into the draft

Strong KPMG deliverables and review patterns are brought forward at the start, so each draft inherits what has worked before.

03

Open points stay visible

Missing support, unresolved judgments, and weak spots stay visible while the narrative is being built, not after a late review round.

KPMG Advantage

Strong prior work shapes the next draft from the start.

Prior KPMG deliverables, working papers, and learned review patterns are carried directly into the next narrative, so teams begin from strong precedent instead of rebuilding from a blank page.

  • Reusable narrative patterns carry forward.
  • Review expectations become visible earlier.
  • Strong work carries forward across engagements.
